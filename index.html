Fela Anikulapo Kuti

1938 - 1997

About the Legendary Afrobeat Artist:

  • 1938 - Olufela Olusegun Oludoton Ransome-Kuti - Fela - was born on the 15th of October, 1938 in Abeokuta, Ogun State
  • 1946 - Fela begins learning the piano, encouraged by his father, who believes studying music is an essential part of a good education.
  • 1958 - He was sent to London to study medicine but decided to study music instead at the Trinity College of Music.
  • 1959 - Fela forms his first group, Fela Ransome-Kuti and His Highlife Rakers. The band record four sides for Melodisc, one of Britain’s first independent African and Caribbean music labels.
  • 1961 - Fela married his first wife - Remilekun Taylor while he was still studying at Trinity College of Music in London.
  • 1962 - Fela graduates from Trinity College of Music. His first son, Femi, is born.
  • 1963 - Fela moved back to Nigeria and trained as a radio producer for the Nigerian Broadcasting Corporation.
  • 1967 - In 1967, he went to Ghana to think up a new musical direction
  • 1969 - He first called his music Afrobeat In 1969.
  • 1969 - Fela discovered the Black Power movement through Sandra Smith (now Sandra Izsadore), a partisan of the Black Panther Party, when he took his band to Los Angeles, US
  • 1970 - Fela formed the Kalakuta Republic, a recording studio, and a home of many people connected to the band.
  • 1972 - Fela renames the Afro-Spot the Africa Shrine and brings it back to life.
  • 1975 - Fela changes his last name from Ransome-Kuti to Anikulapo-Kuti one who carries death in his pouch.
  • 1977 - Fela and the Afrika ’70 released the album Zombie, a scathing attack on Nigerian soldiers using the zombie metaphor to describe the methods of the Nigerian military. The album was a smash hit and infuriated the government, setting off a vicious attack against the Kalakuta Republic, during which one thousand soldiers attacked the commune. Fela was severely beaten, and his elderly mother (whose house was located opposite the commune) was thrown from a window, causing fatal injuries.
  • 1977 - Fela’s response to the attack was to deliver his mother’s coffin to the Dodan Barracks in Lagos, General Olusegun Obasanjo’s residence, and to write two songs, Coffin for Head of State and Unknown Soldier.
  • 1978 - In 1978, Fela married 27 women in a single wedding ceremony. He would eventually divorce them all. He was briefly listed in the Guinness book of records for the most number of women married at one ceremony
  • 1979 - He formed his own political party – MOP. He also ran for president of Nigeria twice.
  • 1984 - Muhammadu Buhari’s government, of which Kuti was a vocal opponent, jailed him on a charge of currency smuggling which Amnesty International and others denounced as politically motivated. Amnesty designated him a prisoner of conscience.
  • 1989 - Fela and Egypt ’80 released the anti-apartheid Beasts of No Nation that depicts on its cover U.S. President Ronald Reagan, UK Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher and South African State President Pieter Willem Botha.
  • 1997 - Dies on the 3rd of August, 1997 in Lagos, at the age of 58. Over a million people gather to pay their final respects.
